About Me

My name's James Philpot. I'm a graphic designer who mainly specializes in Print Design and Project Management, but sometimes does some web stuff too. I've spent the last 10 years as the in-house designer at the Texas Heart Institute in the Texas Medical Center where I've developed a broad set of skills and a tremendous ability to manage multiple projects under tight deadlines. I work well under preassure (thriving under it even) and have a natural ability for leadership and decision making.

I reside in Houston, Texas with my wife and 3 goofy cats, where most of my free time is spent reading sci-fi novels, playing board games, and trying to figure out what's going on in Westworld.

Experience

2009-2013 Texas Heart Institute | Production Assistant
I was brought on to assist on a bi-monthly (6 times a year) medical journal, as well as assist on general print design projects. My duties included:
  • Medical Journal Production: My supervisor and I produced a 100-150 page, medical journal every other month. This included all layout and design, copyediting, and pre-press preperation.
  • Image Evaluation: I was put in charge of evaluating the images submitted by authors and preparing them for print. We typically handled around 1,200 images a year.
  • Digital Illustrations: I tought myself how to use Adobe Illustrator and began creating custom medical illustrations for doctors and publications.
  • General Design Assistance: I helped complete, update, revise, and prepare for press design documents created by my supervisor. These included certificates, brochures, signs, wall cling, and many other printed materials.
2013-2016 Texas Heart Institute | Graphic Design Specialist
I was promoted to Graphic Design Specialist and put in charge of all print design for THI. My tasks included:
  • Logo and Brand Standards: I was the keeper of the brand standards and made sure all uses of our logo adhered to our strict usage guidelines.
  • Print Design: I designed all printed materials for THI including event brochures and signs, wall cling, newspaper ads, flyers and handouts, and donor appeal mailings.
  • Journal Production: The Production Assistant and I still produced the 100+ page bimonthly THI journal.
  • Design Updates: I began making design updates to modernize some of the branded marketing and asthetics. This included a new color scheme and fonts, but did not include a new logo. We started with the yearly donor appeal package and continued through branded department one sheets and media packets.
  • Project Management: I track each phase of our print projects from the initial brainstorm meeting to final printing and mailing to ensure that each phase is on schedule hit the final deadline. Thank god for Gantt charts and whiteboards.
2016-Present Texas Heart Institute | Supervisor, Graphic Design Specialist
I was promoted again to a supervisor role. My duties are much the same as before, but now I also do payroll, employee evaluations, and general mentorship duties. My tasks include:
  • All of the previous entries for Graphic Design Specialist
  • Payroll Management: Oversee timecards and make sure no scheduling conflicts arise. Approve or Deny PTO requests. Finalize timecards and submit to payroll office.
  • Employee Management: Yearly evaluations, conflict resolutions, and general mentorship.
